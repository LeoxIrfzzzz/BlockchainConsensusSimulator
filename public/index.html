Blockchain Glossary

Core Terminology

  • Transaction: A request to record data or transfer value on the network.
  • Block: A permanent digital container of verified transactions.
  • Node: A computer on the network that maintains the ledger.
  • Hash Value: A unique cryptographic fingerprint of the block's data.

Proof of Work (Mining)

  • Nonce: A random number miners guess to alter the block's hash.
  • Brute Forcing: Wasting massive CPU energy to guess millions of nonces until the hash meets the target difficulty.
  • Validation & Adding: The network mathematically verifies the winner's hash, and permanently adds the block.

Proof of Stake (Forging)

  • Validator: A node that locks up collateral (Stake) to participate.
  • Weighted Draw: A low-energy lottery. The more you stake, the higher your chance to be chosen.
  • Forging & Adding: The chosen validator cryptographically signs the block. No energy-intensive mining required.

🛡️ Security Test: The "Zero Stake" Error

If you watch the terminal log at the very end of a simulation, you will notice a red error stating: [PoS] ERROR: Total stake is zero or negative.

This is an intentional security demonstration! To prove the network cannot be spammed by fake participants, the C++ engine deliberately spawns two "Rogue Nodes" with 0 stake and asks them to forge a block. The Proof of Stake algorithm successfully detects them, rejects the forgery, and throws this exact error—proving the network is entirely secure against validators who have no real financial collateral.

🚨 The Avalanche Effect (Tamper Testing)

Try it yourself: Once the blocks are generated, click inside the Data field of any block below and change a letter. You will instantly see its hash recompute and turn Red, proving the data was tampered with. Because each block relies on the previous block's hash, every single block after it will also become invalid! This is how the blockchain remains immutable.
